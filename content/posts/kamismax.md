bodyText: <strong>My Role</strong><br<br><br><strong>My Role</strong><br>Rather than functioning strictly as a front-end developer, my primary contribution was as a <strong>UX/UI designer with hands-on front-end involvement</strong> of the app for stylists, students and teachers. I led the UX direction of the platform, conducting deep user research and defining an experience tailored specifically to stylists, for both learning and uploading. <br><br>I interviewed stylists directly to understand how an e-learning platform should feel for their industry. When I tested references like Udemy or Coursera, the feedback was consistent -  “too academic,” “too serious,” and “not engaging.” Many stylists join the industry right after high school, and traditional learning interfaces can feel intimidating or disconnected from how they prefer to consume content. To design a platform they would genuinely enjoy using, the UX needed to be closer to entertainment rather than education.<br><br>To achieve this, I analyzed design patterns from <strong>Netflix</strong>, <strong>Hulu</strong>, and <strong>Amazon Prime</strong> platforms stylists already interact with daily. <br>Drawing from those insights, I designed a more cinematic, content-forward learning experience, built around visual storytelling, oversized thumbnails, and fluid, intuitive navigation. All UI/UX designs and interactive prototypes were created in Adobe XD, and I facilitated multiple review sessions with stylists to validate interaction patterns, layout hierarchy, and usability across several rounds of feedback.<br><br>In addition to designing the learner-facing experience, I also led the UX and front-end design of the internal admin/settings console, where instructors and content managers upload, organize, and configure training videos. This included reorganizing complex settings into clear, predictable flows, designing video-management dashboards with sortable metadata, and creating intuitive interfaces for tagging, categorizing, and updating content. I also designed step-by-step upload progress states, quality checks, and error-handling patterns—ensuring that non-technical staff could confidently manage large video libraries without confusion. These improvements reduced publishing errors and made the overall content-management workflow significantly smoother.<br><br>Alongside the UX work, I partnered closely with the lead full-stack developer to ensure the experience translated cleanly into implementation. I provided detailed interaction specifications and front-end behavioral guidelines to support development. One feature we co-designed was a multi-camera switching interface for live streams, allowing learners to toggle between angles—crucial for capturing the detailed, close-up techniques stylists need to see but couldn’t access during COVID-era limitations.<br><br>Overall, my role blended UX strategy, UI craftsmanship, and front-end-minded collaboration to create an experience that felt engaging, modern, and tailored to the unique learning style of stylists.<br><br>【日本語】<br>フロントエンド実装だけを担当したわけではなく、主にUX/UIデザイナーとしての役割を担い、UXリサーチからUI設計、フロントエンド寄りの技術検討まで幅広く主導しました。プロフェッショナルな美容師向けに最適化された学習体験を設計するため、リサーチの中心にはスタイリストへのヒアリングを置きました。<br><br>まず既存のeラーニングの例としてUdemyやCourseraを見せたところ、返ってきた声は一貫して「学校っぽい」「真面目すぎる」「ワクワクしない」。多くのスタイリストは高校卒業後すぐに現場に入るため、いわゆる“教育プラットフォーム”のUIは堅苦しく、心理的ハードルが生まれやすいのです。そこで私は「学び」ではなく「エンターテインメント」に近いUXが必要だと結論づけました。<br><br>この仮説をもとに、Netflix・Hulu・Amazon Prime Videoなど、スタイリストが日常的に使う動画サービスのデザインパターンを分析しました。そこから得たインサイトを反映し、ビジュアル中心でシネマライクな学習体験を設計しました。大型サムネイル、ストーリーテリングを意識した情報構造、スムーズなナビゲーションを中心に、直感的で没入感のあるUIを構築しました。UIデザインとプロトタイピングはすべてAdobe XDで行い、複数回のレビューをスタイリストと実施しながら改善を重ねました。<br><br>UX/UI設計に加え、リードフルスタックデベロッパーとも密接に連携し、実装上の制約や技術的実現性を踏まえた仕様の確立にも関わりました。特に、ライブ配信中に複数のカメラアングルを切り替えられるUIは、COVID以降失われた「近距離での技術観察」を補完する重要な機能として、共同で設計したものです。<br><br>結果として、UX戦略・UIデザイン・フロントエンド視点の技術検討を一体化し、スタイリストの学び方にフィットした、現代的で魅力的なプラットフォーム体験を実現しました。
